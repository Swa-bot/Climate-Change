Why Climate Change is NOT Good Change

“At first I thought I was fighting to save rubber trees, then I thought I was fighting to save the Amazon rainforest. Now I realize I am fighting for humanity.”-Chico Mendes

What is Climate Change?

Climate change can essentially be described as the increase in Earth's climate due to an excess of green house gases. Green house gases are water vapour, carbon dioxide, nitrous oxide, and methane. These are called "green house" gases because they trap the sun's energy and radiate it (creating a green house like effect) and heating up the Earth (hence the name "climate change").

Some Anthropogenic Activities Increasing Climate Change:

  • Driving Gasoline-Powered Vehicles-This results in a surplus of CO2 (Carbon Dioxide) due to the gas emissions.
  • Deforestation-Trees absorb a large sum of CO2 in the air so when trees get cut down, there are fewer trees and plants to absorb the CO2.
  • Overconsumption-Over consumption is an incredibly significant cause of not only climate change but also pollution, habitat loss, and much more. Many resources
    are acquired to make even a single product putting a dent
    in the ecosystem and releasing many green house gases in the course of its production.
  • Livestock Farming and Agriculture-Livestock farming and agriculture take up a lot of land especially as the population increases. You might be thinking, when we take up land for agriculture, aren't we planting more plants which helps curb the release of CO2? The answer would be no. This is because trees absorb much more CO2 than plants but take up about the same space (considering the TOTAL land required for agriculture).
